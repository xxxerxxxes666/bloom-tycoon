# Bloom Empire — Habit Psychology & Ethical Retention Audit

## Position

We can use habit psychology to make Bloom Empire more compelling, but we should avoid predatory addiction design. The business goal is **high retention through satisfaction, identity, anticipation, and progress**, not compulsion, shame, or financial pressure.

The useful distinction:

- **Healthy habit loop:** player returns because play feels rewarding, beautiful, and meaningful.
- **Exploitative compulsion loop:** player returns because of anxiety, loss aversion, opaque odds, social pressure, or sunk-cost traps.

Bloom Empire should pursue the first and reject the second. It is better for brand equity, App Store risk, player trust, and long-term LTV.

## Research anchors

- BJ Fogg Behavior Model: behavior occurs when **motivation, ability, and prompt** converge. Source: https://www.behaviormodel.org/
- WHO recognizes gaming disorder as impaired control / priority over other life activities / continuation despite negative consequences. Source: https://www.who.int/standards/classifications/frequently-asked-questions/gaming-disorder
- Apple App Store Review Guidelines require transparent IAP, prohibit misleading mechanics, and scrutinize gambling/loot-box-like systems. Source: https://developer.apple.com/app-store/review/guidelines/
- Academic literature on gaming/gambling-like mechanics highlights risk around variable rewards, loot boxes, near-miss effects, and monetized scarcity. Sources captured during research include NCBI/PMC articles on gaming disorder and loot-box/gambling convergence.

## The hook model translated for Bloom Empire

### 1. Trigger

External prompt or internal desire.

**Safe triggers:**
- Daily boutique reward.
- New order available.
- Greenhouse finished growing.
- Seasonal event milestone ready.
- “Your next upgrade is close.”

**Avoid:**
- Shame notifications.
- Fake emergencies.
- Excessive reminders.
- “You are letting your team down” pressure.

### 2. Action

The player should be able to do one obvious thing quickly.

**Bloom action:**
- Tap Play.
- Complete one flower order.
- Claim a reward.
- Buy one visible upgrade.

Design rule:
> The next action should be obvious within 3 seconds.

### 3. Variable reward

Humans respond strongly to uncertainty, but uncertainty can become predatory when monetized opaquely.

**Safe variable rewards:**
- Chests with visible reward ranges.
- Cosmetic rarity clearly labeled.
- Bonus petals/coins after wins.
- Rare flower discovery from normal play.

**Avoid or delay:**
- Paid randomized loot boxes.
- Opaque odds.
- Near-miss casino wheels.
- Randomized purchases aimed at minors.

### 4. Investment

Player puts something into the product, increasing attachment.

**Bloom investment:**
- Upgraded boutique.
- Decoration choices.
- Flowerpedia collection.
- Customer memory album.
- Streak history.
- Signature bouquet gallery.

Best investment is identity-building, not money pressure.

## Psychological mechanics worth using

### Progress gradient effect

People accelerate when close to a goal.

**Implementation:**
- Show progress toward next shop upgrade.
- Show event milestone progress.
- Show “2 orders until Greenhouse Corner.”
- Give beginner progress bars that start partially filled.

### Goal stacking

Top games make one action advance several goals.

A single level win should advance:
- Level progress.
- Coins.
- Shop upgrade progress.
- Daily reward streak.
- Event track.
- Collection chance.

This feels generous and reduces grind fatigue.

### Endowed progress

Players feel more motivated when already partway through a goal.

**Bloom use:**
- First upgrade meter starts at 60%.
- First daily streak begins with “Day 1 claimed.”
- First Flowerpedia page starts with one discovered flower.

### Zeigarnik / open loop

People remember unfinished goals.

**Bloom use:**
- Show the next locked boutique area.
- Show “Luxury Counter: 340/620 coins.”
- Show incomplete order cards.

**Guardrail:** open loops should invite return, not create anxiety.

### Loss aversion / near miss

Near misses strongly motivate continuation.

**Bloom use:**
- Loss modal says “So close — 1 rose left.”
- Offer retry or earned booster.
- Later: optional extra moves.

**Guardrail:** do not overtune levels to manufacture repeated near-fails too early.

### Collection completion

Completionism drives long-term retention.

**Bloom use:**
- Flowerpedia.
- Vase/ribbon sets.
- Seasonal arrangements.
- Customer postcards.

**Guardrail:** avoid paid-only completion or opaque rare-chasing.

### Identity and ownership

Players retain longer when the world feels like theirs.

**Bloom use:**
- Name the boutique later.
- Choose decor variants.
- Signature arrangement gallery.
- Dark elegant boutique aesthetic as aspirational identity.

### Anticipation and reward ceremony

Dopamine is heavily tied to anticipation, not just reward.

**Bloom use:**
- Chest glow before reveal.
- Upgrade preview before purchase.
- Flower bloom animation before reward count-up.

## Subtle retention systems to implement

### MVP-safe

1. **Daily reward streak**
   - Reward increases for consecutive claim days.
   - Missed day resets gently or pauses; no shame.
   - Preview tomorrow’s reward.

2. **Upgrade progress meter**
   - Always show distance to next boutique upgrade.

3. **Next-order tease**
   - Completing one order reveals the next customer/order type.

4. **Win/loss modal**
   - Win advances multiple visible tracks.
   - Loss highlights remaining objective and offers retry.

5. **Collection skeleton**
   - Start with visible Flowerpedia slots, not monetized packs.

### Later

1. Seasonal Bloom Pass.
2. Limited-time flower festivals.
3. Social garden club.
4. Friend gifting.
5. Event chests with disclosed reward ranges.
6. Cosmetic bundles.

## Anti-predatory guardrails

Bloom Empire should avoid:

- Paid randomized loot boxes in early product.
- Hidden odds.
- Aggressive scarcity timers.
- Forced ads.
- Multiple purchase modals chained together.
- Purchase buttons near close buttons.
- Currency obfuscation that hides real cost.
- Shame-based notifications.
- Infinite escalation of losses to sell recovery.
- Children-targeted pressure mechanics.

## Recommendation

Implement habit architecture in this order:

1. Daily reward streak.
2. Upgrade progress meter.
3. Win/loss modal with near-win clarity.
4. Reward chest ceremony.
5. Flowerpedia collection skeleton.
6. Event progress track.
7. Optional rewarded ads only after core retention is proven.

This gives us the psychological force of the greats while preserving a premium Xerxes Florals brand posture.
